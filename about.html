About Us

Savanna Junior Care Foundation

Mission Statement: Savanna Junior Care Foundation’s mission is to support and empower African children and adolescents to overcome adversity and reach their full potential by providing innovative, evidence-based, and community-driven solutions in education, health, and economic empowerment.

Vision Statement: We envision a world where every African child and adolescent thrives, empowered with knowledge, skills, and opportunities to lead fulfilling and impactful lives.

History: In 2020, our founder created a neighborhood bible club, starting with seven children and one volunteer teacher in a clergy's living room. By 2022, the club evolved into the Savanna Junior Care Foundation, pivoting to support children and adolescents during the COVID-19 pandemic. Over time, we expanded our focus to suburban outreaches, physical support, talk therapy, and teaching moral values. Officially registered in 2024, we’ve run four initiatives reaching over 500 children and adolescents.

Core Values:

  • Empowerment: Equipping children and adolescents with tools to unlock their potential and thrive.
  • Integrity: Transparency, honesty, and accountability in every action we take.
  • Collaboration: Building meaningful partnerships for lasting impact.
  • Compassion: Addressing the needs of vulnerable children with care and respect.
  • Innovation: Embracing evidence-based solutions to overcome challenges and inspire change.
  • Inclusion: Creating equal opportunities for every child.
  • Excellence: Committing to the highest standards in all programs and initiatives.

Over the next two years, we aim to scale programs across underserved communities, establish a robust monitoring framework, and launch school-based projects, starting with a pilot at Ojoo High School. Additionally, we will establish a Youth Resource Hub, providing students with tools and guidance for academic, personal, and career growth.
