About Us

Empowering Africa’s Youth Through Compassion and Community

At Savanna Junior Foundation, we envision a world where every African child and adolescent thrives. Through a compassionate, community-driven, and innovative approach, we support endangered youth by creating access to education, health resources, and economic opportunities.

Our mission is to empower African children and adolescents, fostering hope, resilience, and growth. We collaborate with local communities, NGOs, government agencies, and advocates to create sustainable, impactful change.

Success Stories

Stories of Impact from Volunteers and Children

“What’s Love Got to Do with Breakfast?’ has been a life-changing experience for our students. It helped them heal from emotional wounds, redefine their understanding of love and relationships, and prioritize self-love and self-worth. The sessions provided a safe space to process emotions, confront fears and embrace the power of Agape love. Our students are now better informed, emotionally stable"

Ayodele Olubunmi
Principal, Crown Heights College, Ibadan, Nigeria. Beneficiary of Resilience Mentorship Program

"In their dedication to supporting the community, they have extended their charitable effort to benefit Solutions to Our Generation, Mission Orphanage. Recently, we had the pleasure of receiving them on a visit to our orphanage, bearing gifts to the excitement of the children. Their visits have fostered a wonderful friendship, particularly between Mr. Emmanuel Udofia, the founder of the foundation, and the children at our home. We are grateful for this connection and look forward to further collaborations in the future."

Oluwaseyifunmi Favour
Director, Solutions to Our Generation Mission Orphanage Beneficiary of Orphanage Support Program

"I dropped out of school because I was struggling. I repeated a class. Every day I walked 5 kilometers to school and 5 kilometers back. I don’t eat when going to school. That time, I was 19 years old. My mother is a widow and it was not easy to take care of seven of us. But Savanna Junior Foundation registered me in another school that is not that far. They paid the money for the school, bought me school things and a new school uniform. They even gave me money for food everyday. Two aunties usually came to our house to talk to me and encourage me. They also helped my mother to change her shop. Her former shop was leaking and she was not making money there. But they paid for a new shop and paid half of the rent. Now, my mother is making more money there. "

Favour Umeh
Beneficiary of School Back-to-School Program
