Empowerment Programs

Goal

The Empowerment Programs focus on equipping adolescents and young adults with vocational and entrepreneurial skills that can help them become economically independent. These programs target youth from economically disadvantaged backgrounds who may not have access to opportunities that can break the cycle of poverty. By providing them with the necessary training and support, we empower them to create income-generating opportunities for themselves and their families.

Target Audience

Adolescents and young adults: from underprivileged backgrounds, especially those who are unemployed or underemployed.

Parents: who need support in starting or growing their small businesses to better provide for their families.

Small business owners:who require support to scale their businesses and improve profitability.

Impact

Vocational training and skills acquisition: The program offers training in areas like tailoring, carpentry, digital marketing, coding, and other marketable skills that can help youth start their own businesses or gain employment.

Business support: For small business owners, the program provides resources and mentorship on how to grow their businesses. This includes assistance with business plans, marketing strategies, and financial management.

Economic independence: By equipping youth with practical skills and business knowledge, the program helps reduce their vulnerability to unemployment and exploitation and enables them to support themselves and contribute to their communities.
